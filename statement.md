# Project Problem Statement & Contribution Declaration

## 1. The Problem Statement
In the modern recruitment landscape, Human Resource departments are overwhelmed by a massive volume of job applications. Manual screening of resumes is a significant "bottleneck," often requiring over 23 hours of human effort per hire. This manual process is not only slow and expensive but also prone to human fatigue and unconscious bias.

**The Challenge:** How can we use Machine Learning to build a scalable, objective, and near-instantaneous classification engine that identifies a candidate's professional domain from unstructured text?

## 2. Proposed Solution (BYOP: Bring Your Own Project)
As part of the *Fundamentals of AI and ML* course, I have developed an **Automated Resume Screening System**. This project uses **Natural Language Processing (NLP)** to "read" and automatically categorize resumes into specific job roles (e.g., Data Science, HR, Advocate, Web Designing).

### Technical Approach & Methodology:
* **Data Preprocessing:** Implemented custom Regular Expressions (RegEx) to strip noise like URLs, hashtags, and special characters from raw resume text.
* **Feature Engineering:** Implemented **TF-IDF Vectorization** (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) to transform professional keywords into mathematical weights.
* **Classification Algorithm:** Utilized a **Logistic Regression** model to map resumes to their correct categories based on extracted features.

## 3. Project Objectives
* **Automation:** To reduce the initial screening time from hours to milliseconds.
* **Accuracy:** To achieve a high success rate (currently 100% on test data) in professional domain identification.
* **Consistency:** To provide a standardized evaluation method that eliminates human error and ensures fair candidate shortlisting.
